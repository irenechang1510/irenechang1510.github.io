Research

  • Tufts University

    Sep 2022 – Dec 2022

    Domain Adaptation for Mental Workload Classification

    Summary: Brain signals such as fNIRS vary significantly from one person to another, thus building customized brain-computer interfaces for every user is a necessary, yet laborious thing to do. As part of the ongoing expansion of transfer learning models that can serve this purpose, this study seeks to evaluate a style transfer mapping (STM) method on a dataset of fNIRS signals in a mental workload intensity level classification task. We show that the accuracy of STM varies greatly on the target test data due to the shift in distributions between the train and test set. In addition, we also assess the performance of a modified version of the original model and show that the algorithm with reduced complexity can perform just as well.

  • Tufts University

    Sep 2022 – May 2023

    Domain Adaptation for Bridge Damage Detection

    Summary: Bridges in real life vary in structures and materials, which have given rise to many studies on transfer learning models that can be trained on a given bridge to analyze the sensor signals of a new bridge. This study seeks to evaluate a transfer learning technique called Joint Distribution Adaptation on sensor data of acceleration measurements in a binary damage classification task. This technique has previously been showed to produced improved precision on a similar task. We analyze the strengths and drawbacks of our model, with a view to guiding the next steps of this study in the direction of developing a generalizable, trustworthy classifier for damage detection on bridges.
